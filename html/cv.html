PROJECTS

  • HDF-MambaVAD: Hybrid Framework for Unsupervised Video Anomaly Detection (Sep 2025 – Apr 2026):
    Role: Research Student — Research manuscript in preparation
    • Developed HDF-MambaVAD, a two-stage unsupervised video anomaly detection framework combining optical-flow reconstruction and future-frame prediction for object-centric surveillance videos.
    • Redesigned the optical-flow reconstruction stage with Disentangled Flow Encoding, Magnitude Gating Fusion, Vector Quantisation bottleneck, and progressive decoding to learn stable normal-motion representations.
    • Built a dual-branch VMamba-based U-Net that processes RGB frames and reconstructed optical flow separately, then fuses appearance and motion features using Iterative Attentional Feature Fusion at the bottleneck.
    • Integrated Lite CONASkip, a lightweight context-aware skip-routing mechanism, to reduce direct encoder-to-decoder feature leakage and amplify prediction errors on suspicious motion patterns.
    • Evaluated the framework on UCSD Ped2, CUHK Avenue, and ShanghaiTech, achieving frame-level AUC scores of 98.57%, 92.50%, and 74.07%, respectively.
